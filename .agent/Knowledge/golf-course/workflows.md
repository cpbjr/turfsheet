# Golf Course Maintenance Workflows

## Daily Operations

### Morning Setup (Pre-Play)
**Timeframe:** Before first tee time (typically 6:00-7:00 AM)

**Tasks:**
1. Move cups on greens (new hole locations)
2. Mow greens (if scheduled)
3. Rake bunkers
4. Set tee markers
5. Fill ball washers
6. Check irrigation system
7. Clear debris from course

**Staff Required:** 2-4 crew members
**Duration:** 1-2 hours

### Mowing Operations
**Frequency:** Varies by area

**Greens:**
- Daily during growing season
- Early morning (before play)
- Precision equipment
- Cleanup edges and approaches

**Tees:**
- 2-3 times per week
- Can be done during play (low impact)

**Fairways:**
- 2-3 times per week
- Early morning or late afternoon
- May require course closure for safety

**Rough:**
- Weekly or bi-weekly
- Can be done during play
- Less precision required

### Chemical Applications
**Planning:** Weather-dependent

**Process:**
1. Check weather forecast (wind, rain)
2. Prepare equipment and materials
3. Mark application areas
4. Apply chemicals (early morning preferred)
5. Post signage (regulatory requirement)
6. Document application (what, where, when, how much)
7. Clean equipment

**Record Keeping:**
- Product name and EPA registration
- Application rate
- Total area covered
- Weather conditions
- Applicator name
- Date and time

## Weekly Operations

### Equipment Maintenance
**Schedule:** End of week or as needed

**Tasks:**
1. Sharpen mower blades
2. Clean equipment
3. Check fluid levels
4. Inspect for damage
5. Minor repairs

**Staff:** Equipment manager + 1-2 assistants
**Duration:** Half-day to full day

### Irrigation System
**Schedule:** Weekly review, daily adjustments

**Tasks:**
1. Walk system for leaks/breaks
2. Adjust zones based on weather
3. Test coverage patterns
4. Clean nozzles/filters
5. Document water usage

### Course Walk
**Schedule:** Weekly (Superintendent + Assistant)

**Purpose:**
- Identify problem areas
- Plan upcoming work
- Check staff performance
- Note player feedback areas

**Output:** Task list for next week

## Seasonal Operations

### Spring Transition
**Timing:** March-April (temperature dependent)

**Major Tasks:**
1. **Aeration**
   - Core greens, tees, fairways
   - Remove cores or topdress
   - 1-2 week recovery time

2. **Overseeding**
   - Apply seed to thin areas
   - Requires consistent moisture
   - Limit traffic during germination

3. **Fertilization**
   - Spring feeding program
   - Slow-release products
   - Multiple applications

4. **Equipment Prep**
   - Full maintenance on all units
   - Sharpen/replace reels
   - Replace worn parts

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks
**Impact:** Course may have reduced conditions during transition

### Summer Maintenance
**Timing:** June-August

**Focus:**
1. **Irrigation Management**
   - Daily adjustments based on ET (evapotranspiration)
   - Hand watering hot spots
   - Monitoring for overwatering

2. **Disease Prevention**
   - Fungicide applications
   - Monitoring turf health
   - Adjusting practices to reduce stress

3. **Mowing Frequency**
   - Increased during peak growth
   - May mow greens twice daily
   - Adjust HOC based on stress

**Challenges:**
- High heat stress
- Increased play traffic
- Disease pressure
- Water restrictions (possible)

### Fall Preparation
**Timing:** September-October

**Major Tasks:**
1. **Overseeding** (warm-to-cool transition climates)
   - Apply cool-season grass seed
   - Reduce competition from existing turf
   - Maintain moisture for germination

2. **Aeration** (second round)
   - Less disruptive than spring
   - Prepares for winter dormancy

3. **Equipment Maintenance**
   - End-of-season servicing
   - Storage preparation
   - Budget planning for replacements

4. **Course Improvements**
   - Tree work
   - Drainage projects
   - Infrastructure repairs

**Duration:** 6-8 weeks

### Winter Operations
**Timing:** November-March

**Focus:**
1. **Equipment Overhaul**
   - Full maintenance on all units
   - Major repairs
   - Painting/refurbishment

2. **Course Projects**
   - Tree removal
   - Bunker renovations
   - Cart path repairs
   - Drainage improvements

3. **Reduced Maintenance**
   - Minimal mowing (dormant turf)
   - Monitor for disease
   - Protect greens from traffic/frost

**Staff:** Reduced crew (off-season scheduling)

## Project Management

### Long-Term Projects
**Examples:**
- Bunker renovations
- Drainage installation
- Irrigation system upgrades
- Tree management programs

**Workflow:**
1. **Planning**
   - Define scope
   - Budget approval
   - Timeline creation
   - Material sourcing

2. **Execution**
   - Break into phases
   - Assign staff/contractors
   - Track progress
   - Document work

3. **Completion**
   - Final inspection
   - Turf establishment
   - Budget reconciliation
   - Post-project review

**Tracking:**
- Task boards (Kanban style)
- Photo documentation
- Budget tracking
- Timeline adherence

## Emergency Responses

### Weather Events
**Storm Damage:**
1. Safety assessment
2. Debris removal
3. Drainage issues
4. Tree damage
5. Irrigation repairs

**Drought:**
1. Water conservation measures
2. Priority areas (greens, tees)
3. Reduce mowing frequency
4. Adjust fertilization
5. Monitor turf health

**Frost/Freeze:**
1. Delay play until thaw
2. Protect greens from traffic
3. Adjust irrigation timing
4. Monitor for ice damage

### Equipment Failures
**Critical Equipment Down:**
1. Assess repair vs. rental
2. Adjust schedule to compensate
3. Communicate with staff
4. Document downtime/costs
5. Prevent backlog

## Communication Workflows

### Staff Communication
**Daily:**
- Morning meeting (task assignments)
- Mid-day check-ins (adjust as needed)
- End-of-day report (completed tasks)

**Weekly:**
- Upcoming work review
- Equipment needs
- Supply requests
- Safety reminders

### Management Communication
**Reports:**
- Daily task completion
- Weekly summaries
- Monthly budget reviews
- Seasonal planning

**Escalation:**
- Budget overruns
- Equipment failures
- Weather impacts
- Safety incidents

---

*This document captures common workflows. Specific courses may have variations based on climate, grass types, and operational preferences.*
