# Golf Course Terminology

## Course Areas

### Greens
- **Definition:** Putting surfaces where the hole is located
- **Characteristics:** Shortest grass, highest maintenance requirements
- **HOC (Height of Cut):** Typically 0.125" - 0.156"

### Fairways
- **Definition:** Mowed areas between tee and green
- **Characteristics:** Medium-length grass, regular mowing
- **HOC:** Typically 0.375" - 0.625"

### Rough
- **Definition:** Unmowed or lightly mowed areas bordering fairways
- **Characteristics:** Longer grass, penalty area for errant shots
- **Types:** Primary rough, secondary rough

### Tees
- **Definition:** Starting areas for each hole
- **Characteristics:** Durable turf, regular divot repair
- **HOC:** Similar to fairways

### Bunkers
- **Definition:** Sand hazards
- **Maintenance:** Raking, edging, sand replacement

## Equipment & Operations

### HOC (Height of Cut)
- **Definition:** The height at which grass is mowed
- **Units:** Measured in inches or fractions of an inch
- **Importance:** Critical for turf health and playability

### Mowing Patterns
- **Direction:** N/S, E/W, diagonal
- **Rotation:** Changing patterns to prevent grain
- **Cleanup:** Removing clippings from edges

### Equipment Types
- **Greens Mowers:** Precision reel mowers for putting surfaces
- **Fairway Mowers:** Larger units for broader areas
- **Rough Mowers:** Rotary or reel for longer grass
- **Trimmers/Edgers:** Detail work around obstacles

## Maintenance Concepts

### Irrigation
- **Zones:** Different areas with different water needs
- **Timing:** Early morning preferred
- **Coverage:** Ensuring even distribution

### Chemical Applications
- **Fertilizers:** Nutrient management
- **Pesticides:** Disease and pest control
- **Herbicides:** Weed management
- **Record Keeping:** Required for regulatory compliance

### Agronomic Health
- **Soil Testing:** pH, nutrients, compaction
- **Aeration:** Core or solid tine
- **Topdressing:** Sand application to smooth/improve soil
- **Overseeding:** Adding grass seed to improve density

## Scheduling

### Daily Tasks
- **Morning Setup:** Course preparation before play
- **Mowing:** Greens, tees, fairways (rotation)
- **Bunker Maintenance:** Raking, edging
- **Detail Work:** Trimming, cleanup

### Weekly Tasks
- **Irrigation Adjustments:** Based on weather
- **Equipment Maintenance:** Cleaning, sharpening
- **Chemical Applications:** As scheduled

### Seasonal Tasks
- **Spring:** Aeration, overseeding, fertilization
- **Summer:** Irrigation management, disease prevention
- **Fall:** Preparation for dormancy
- **Winter:** Equipment overhaul, course improvements

## Staff Roles

### Superintendent
- **Responsibilities:** Overall course management, budgeting, agronomic decisions
- **Authority:** Final say on maintenance operations

### Assistant Superintendent
- **Responsibilities:** Day-to-day operations, staff management
- **Authority:** Operational decisions

### Equipment Manager
- **Responsibilities:** Equipment maintenance, repairs, inventory
- **Authority:** Equipment-related decisions

### Crew Members
- **Responsibilities:** Executing daily maintenance tasks
- **Specializations:** Greens crew, fairway crew, irrigation

## Rotation Systems

### Blue/Orange Rotation (19-day cycle)
- **Purpose:** Staffing pattern for consistent coverage
- **Mechanics:** Two groups alternating work/off days
- **Benefits:** Predictable schedules, coverage optimization
